Notable Achievements

Louvre Exhibition

In 2009, Hirohiko Araki’s artwork was showcased at the Louvre Museum in Paris as part of the “Le Louvre invite la bande dessinée” exhibition. This rare honor recognized JoJo’s artistic brilliance.

The display highlighted Araki’s detailed illustrations, blending manga with classical art influences, and drew international attention to the series.

Awards and Recognition

JoJo has won prestigious awards, including the Japan Media Arts Festival Award. Its innovative storytelling and art have earned critical acclaim across the globe.

The anime adaptations have also received accolades, boosting the series’ reputation in both manga and animation circles.

Longevity and Popularity

Running since 1987, JoJo boasts over 30 years of continuous publication. With over 100 million copies sold, it remains a beloved franchise with a global fanbase.

Its adaptability—spanning manga, anime, and games—ensures its enduring relevance in pop culture.
